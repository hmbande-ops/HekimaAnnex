Welcome to Hekima Academy Annex

Hekima Academy Annex is committed to providing quality education that nurtures the holistic development of our students.

We create a safe and stimulating learning environment where every child can thrive academically, socially, and emotionally.

Curriculum at Hekima Academy Annex

Hekima Academy follows the Competency-Based Education (CBE) curriculum

The curriculum focuses on developing learners’ skills, values, knowledge, and talents through a child-centered and practical learning approach.

Kindergarten (Pre-Primary: PP1 & PP2)
  • Play-based and activity-oriented learning.
  • Development of early literacy and numeracy skills.
  • Learning areas include language activities, mathematics, environmental studies, creative arts, music, and physical movement.
  • Focus on social, emotional, and moral development.
  • Assessment through continuous observation.
Junior Primary (Grades 1–3)
  • Strong foundation in literacy, numeracy, and communication skills.
  • Subjects include English, Kiswahili, Indigenous Language, Mathematics, Environmental Studies, Creative Arts, Religious Education, and Physical & Health Education.
  • Integration of digital literacy and hands-on learning.
  • Continuous assessment in place of high-stakes examinations.
  • Encourages creativity, collaboration, and problem-solving.
Junior High School (Grades 7–9)
  • Broad-based curriculum preparing learners for future career pathways.
  • Core subjects include English, Kiswahili, Mathematics, Integrated Science, Social Studies, and Religious Education.
  • Optional subjects based on learner interests such as Business Studies, Agriculture, Computer Science, Home Science, Arts, and Foreign Languages.
  • Emphasis on critical thinking, talent identification, and community service learning.
  • Assessment through projects, practical work, and written evaluations.

Achievements

Our students consistently perform well academically and participate actively in co-curricular activities.

Hekima Academy participated in her first Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) in 2014.
The school has recorded applaudable results in its KCPE since with the mean score rising as follows; 308 in 2014, 329 in 2015, 326 in 2016 and 352 in 2017.

Most of our pupils have been admitted to some of the best performing high schools in the country. Notably Kakamega High School, Kitui High School, Lugulu Girls National School, Lodwar Boys High School, Chesamisi High School, Kamosi Girls High School, Butere Girls High School among others.
